Radar Game

One of the sizes specified for Ludum Dare mini #34 “Aspect” was a 500px-diameter circle.

This immediately brought to mind a radar.

Making a radar sweep animation is really trivial.  Here’s mine:

(I made the blobs a bit bigger and brighter for the video because video compression really hates subtlety)

I represented the display as a square gray-scale bitmap in CPU memory.

Each frame updates a pie-slice between the sweep angle of the previous frame and the new frame.  All blobs in that pie-slice are drawn into this bitmap by the CPU using a simple radiant fill circle for their intensity.

This bitmap is uploaded each frame.

And drawn with a simple GLSL fragment shader which uses the polar angle of the pixel and the current sweep to determine the intensity of that pixel.

I had this working in surprisingly short time, and I started experimenting with moving around.  I imagined a speed-boat and an archipelago.  I’ve played with line-of-sight before so I imagined simple height-map terrain being used to hide - or not hide - helicopters and such.

I got nice coastline effect and other vessels and such; sadly didn’t make a newer video of it the final state though.

There are some interesting game dynamics when your only screen is a radar screen.  Should objects move when not in sweep and you move, for example?  And what should happen if you turn?  I tried to think back to my brief but memorable trips on MTB-102 when I’d stared at the sweep.

I just had trouble getting the sweep fast enough to make it a fast-paced game without seeming actually too fast.  The scale is just all wrong.

Anyway, I never did actually spend enough time to enter the miniLD31.  But four or so radar games did turn up!
