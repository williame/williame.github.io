Will Edwards
programming

I have always loved programming - its like Lego without gravity.

Basic on my ZX81 graduating to assembler and Turbo Pascal during my teens.

Developed phone OS software - engineer, architect, product manager - but got made irrelevant by the iPhone and redundant by Android.

These days I mostly work with data, big data and fitting big data onto small boxes.

After a xmas break - guests do get in the way - it was time to get back to the robot!

To be honest I’ve been putting off etching the motor controller card.  I really don’t like the whole idea of playing around with acid and stuff.  My recollections of etching (with crayons) from high-school are vague at best so I’m a rank beginner.

The UV lightbox was, well, a desk lamp with the UV bulb.  Its not at all like the sun-tanning beds you see in the pictures.  I pointed it roughly in the direction of the board, and put a kitchen-foil-lined box over it to keep me safe.

My first attempts were a dismal failure.  After 10 minutes exposure there were faint lines visible after I put it through 10 minutes of developer.  I didn’t know if this was normal or otherwise, so it was onwards to the acid.  Nothing at all came off.  Nothing.  Hmm.  I speculated that it was underexposed.

Back under the light for half an hour, moving the light around slightly a couple of times to even it out, and the mask was much more prominent.  Clearly the 3-10 minute guidelines were based on fancy light boxes with neat racks of UV tubes :)

It was very exciting to see the circuit appear after just a few minutes in the acid the second time.  However, after a good half hour there was still no budging some copper along to the top edge of the board.  I speculate that it was underexposed by the unevenness of my lamp coverage.

We have a spare board but I’m out of acid.  I think this board can be scratched into shape so I’ll try and get it used.

Now I’ll hand over the card for my co-conspirator to tidy, drill and solder and test.
