Ludum Dare #23 approaches

The 10th anniversary of the competition and they’re gunning for over 1000 entries!

So what game are you making this weekend?

I’ll be entering again with my artist buddy and anyone who wants to join us (#glest-ludumdare on irc.freenode.net - wait there for one of us to turn up).  We’ve got a general concept worked out ahead of time - the kind of game we imagine making, the technology choice - and we’ll just have to see if we can bend it to the theme when its announced.

If the theme is Artificial Intelligence, here’s an idea you can have for free:

Its Turing’s 100th birthday.  He is of course famous for inventing the Turing Test, the standard test for judging artificial intelligence.

So you, dear Ludum Dare competitor, make a website that lets players talk to alternatively a robot you’ve written or another human player and they have to vote on whether their peer is human or robot.

So far, sounds daunting making that robot?

Not at all!  Don’t make one.  Always match humans with humans.

And, for half the conversations, randomly slightly mess up some replies.  You might swap them with another conversation, or introduce some mistakes, or such.

Record whether people are fooled into thinking they are talking to this super clever artificial intelligence you claim to have created.

Regardless of how well this does on Ludum Dare itself, it’d make super cool reading when you write it up for your blog!
