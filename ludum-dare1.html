Played Cage Flight yet?  Its a multi-player on-line space-fighter game!  You are in a cage, weaving around trying to shoot down the other players; distilled survival of the fittest!

Its the game we wanted to play, so we made it!  We are super pleased and proud of it.  We didn’t settle on what we would make until the theme was announced; I had hoped we’d fly around a fractal landscape and put a lot of preparation time into learning ray-tracing.  In the end I think we shoe-horned the theme Evolution into it acceptably and got the very best game we’ve made to boot.

Ludum Dare 24 contest approaches

Yippee, its time again!  We are planning a Jam contest entry.  As you can see, our artist has been sharpening his pencils and doing team building pictures.  I think the ears and sandals chap is me.

Previously we’ve tried to show that platform games don’t need to be made from blocks and tiles, and that text adventures can be pretty.  Neither wowed the audiences sadly.  I really rather liked the text adventure particularly, but then maybe I’m a bit stuck in the 90s with my gaming preferences.  Without Internet access games were supposed to be deep and thoughtful or pushing the boundaries (like Harpoon and flight sims; making you think your computer could do what the big computers did in the Cold War).

But nobody really plays sims any more.  Not even SimCity.  Ludum Dare is full of platform games and 3D shooters dominate the real marketplace.

We keep coming back towards RTS.  We have a history of doing Glest mods - and Tiberian Sun mods and modding tools if you go back far enough - so its natural that we think RTS is cool.  But it’d be hard to make an RTS that is popular, I think.  Its a niche.

This time I think we’re definitely going Javascript webGL.  Its maturing enough to be a good enough platform.  It means no heavy computation, but that’s fine.  It has a few secrets; websockets, for example.  The Web Audio API isn’t really there but works well enough in Chrome to be usable in LD.

Just a few days to go.  If you want to join our team, do let me know!

Ludum Dare #23 starts! Tiny World

And the theme is “Tiny World”.

To be honest, I’m a bit stumped.  Teammates will be arriving on-line soon and I don’t have much concrete overarching vision for this theme, which was my job and why I got up at 3am.

I really want to stay away from actually making a platform game where the map is circular and smallish.

So, in the text adventure vein:

  • is it that the obligatory mad professor has built a microscope instead of telescope?  That he is building mechanical brass-cog nano-bots?  That they are slowly transforming the world as you break the puzzles?
  • is it that everyone knows everyone else?  That there’s some secret (treasure) that increasingly silly teams of people keep turning up to recover, and you have to outsmart them all?  When a vicar and his wife arrive, followed by some Sioux in a canoe, and everyone seems to know everyone else and the pirates are on first name terms with everyone except the swiss mountaineer whom they have previously had a disagreement with, then you can say it truly is a small world?
  • And what’s the world called?  Aardvark would be a strategic name from an alphabetical sorting perspective ;)

All ideas welcome!
